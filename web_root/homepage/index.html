About Me

Background

My name is Jacob Flowers, and I have been taking classes here at TCC for a little over a year now. Growing up, I always had a wild imagination, and this was evident in my hobbies, ranging from drawing, to painting, to video game creation, to writing my own stories. Pretty much everything I did for fun involved me getting my creative juices flowing. Once I hit my high school years, I slowly started to abandon physical media and gravitated more to digital media. My artistic ability with a pencil just could not keep up with what I would envision in my head, so I searched for other ways to create art.

During the Covid lockdown in 2020, me and my mother found a website called Outschool, which had a wide variety of elective classes that students of all ages could sign up for. They were small, and would only last for like a month or so, we figured it would be a good way to explore some of my interests. Around summertime, I took a class that introduced me to a neat little software called Photoshop. Initially, it was a bit overwhelming, but I quickly learned the basics and was soon having a blast doing all sorts of crazy things to photos. A few months later, I was searching for potential jobs for my future. I then stumbled upon Graphic Design. Wait, you're telling me I could use software similar to Photoshop for a living? AND make decent money doing so? At the age of 16, I decided right there and then that I wanted to be a Graphic Design major.

Fast forward to now, and I do not regret my decision at all. My time here at TCC's Visual Arts Center has had it's share of ups and downs, but I feel like I have grown so much as a designer in such a short amount of time.

Goals

My main goal as of right now is to buckle down and get good grades for the rest of my classes so I can graduate with my Associates Degree in the spring. After that... well I'm still undecided. I'm not sure if I should attempt to transfer and get a Bachelors Degree, or if I should dive straight into the workforce and apply for a job. That's another thing: I'm not 100% sure on what exactly it is that I want to do in the Graphic Design field. I enjoy pretty much everything. Whether it's logo creation, designing posters and book covers, clothing design, or whatever else, I have fun doing all of it. Even animation has begun to grow on me over the past few weeks as I've learned how to use Adobe After Effects. I guess it gives me a lot of options for my future, but that is both a blessing and a curse as it leaves me feeling a bit lost on where to go next. At some point, I need to sit down and really narrow down what exactly I want to do next. The spring will be here before I know it, there's not a whole lot of time left.

During my time as an artist and as a designer, I have definitely been inspired by various creators and their artstyles. As recently as last year, I've learned about Aaron Draplin and was not only impressed with his work, but also his approach to the design process as a whole. I would definitely love to meet him someday and pick his brain to learn some things. Another person who I have admired for the longest time without even knowing it is a man named Yoichi Kotabe, a character designer who animated and illustrated artwork for various companies, including Nintendo. As somebody who grew up playing a lot of Mario games, I was often influenced by the cartoony style of these games and would try to replicate that style in my own works. As I've grown older, I have tried to branch out and experiment with different styles, but his influence still can be found in some of my artwork even to this day.
